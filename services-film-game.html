Common Questions

Everything You Need to Know

How long does a typical film or game score session take?

+

Session length depends on the score's complexity and the size of the ensemble. A standard day runs 8–10 hours with union breaks. Smaller indie projects often wrap in one or two days; major studio scores may span several sessions across a week or more. We'll estimate your session timeline during the quote process based on your minute count and orchestration.

Can I direct the session remotely?

+

Absolutely — and it's our most popular option. Our remote scoring setup includes Source-Connect Pro, ListenTo, secure 4K multi-camera video from the stage, and real-time talkback directly to the conductor. Composers and directors have run sessions from LA, London, Tokyo, and beyond without ever setting foot in Prague. We handle the time-zone logistics too.

What does the buyout include?

+

All MUSA recordings are 100% buyout: orchestra contracting, conductor, studio engineers, venue, music preparation, session recording, mixing, and stem delivery. You receive total IP ownership in perpetuity with no residuals, no union backend, and no ongoing licensing fees. The contract is straightforward. What you pay is all you pay.

Can you handle audio mixes as well?

+

Yes. We deliver stems and mixed sessions in whatever format your post-production pipeline requires — stereo or 5.1. Our engineers work closely with your mix team to ensure the orchestral sessions translate cleanly into your final audio format. Just let us know your delivery specs upfront and we'll record and route accordingly.

What is the typical lead time?

+

Our typical lead time is 2–6 weeks from confirmed booking to session day, which covers orchestra contracting, venue securing, and music preparation. We've accommodated rush projects in under two weeks when schedules align. The earlier you reach out, the more flexibility we have on dates, venue choice, and ensemble size. Don't wait until your picture lock — contact us as soon as your timeline is roughly known.
